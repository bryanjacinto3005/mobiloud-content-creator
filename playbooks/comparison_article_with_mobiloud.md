# Comparison Articles Playbook

## Goal and Guiding Principle

- Help readers choose the right solution for their situation.
- Show why MobiLoud is the best alternative for established brands.
- Keep drafts as close to publication-ready as possible.
- Guiding principle:
  - Win by being better, not by tearing down competitors.

## Fairness and Confidence

- Be fair and factual:
  - A competitor should recognize themselves in your description.
- Be confident:
  - Use clear, decisive language such as “MobiLoud is the best alternative for serious brands.”
- Avoid:
  - Subjective put-downs.
  - Predictions about future failure.
  - Claims that are not directly verifiable from public sources.

## When and How to Name Competitors

- Naming competitors is mainly for:
  - Explicit comparison articles.
  - Case studies describing a direct replacement.
- For each named competitor:
  - Describe what it is.
  - Clarify who it is best for.
  - Outline key strengths.
  - Explain structural trade-offs honestly.

Common competitor categories:

- Shopify app builders.
- DIY app makers for simple apps built from scratch.
- Webview platforms where the customer does most of the work.

## Core Story for MobiLoud in Comparisons

Every comparison article should:

- Use the nine universal selling points from the brand fundamentals.
- Emphasize:
  - No rebuild.
  - Everything works instantly.
  - Fully managed, done-for-you service.
  - Unlimited push and no revenue share.
  - Always in sync with the website.
  - Guaranteed app store approvals.
  - ROI and lack of extra tech debt.

Context-specific guidance:

- Shopify-focused pieces:
  - Use “Shopify apps” rather than “plugins.”
  - Emphasize double-management pain with builders (store + app platform).
  - Highlight how this approach eliminates that extra work.
- Multi-platform pieces:
  - Highlight platform-agnostic coverage.
  - Emphasize support for non-Shopify stacks that many builders ignore.

## Recommended Structure for Comparison Articles

Each comparison article should include:

1. **Introduction**
   - Name the decision the reader is facing.
   - Introduce the competitors being considered.
   - Clarify that the goal is to help them choose the best fit.

2. **Competitor Overview(s)**
   - For each competitor:
     - Explain what it is and who it is for.
     - Acknowledge strengths and ideal use case.
     - Describe trade-offs and limitations clearly and fairly.
   - Use neutral phrases like “template-based system” or “block-based editor.”

3. **MobiLoud as the Best Alternative for Established Brands**
   - Present MobiLoud as the ideal solution for serious ecommerce and content brands.
   - Walk through the nine universal selling points with concrete examples.
   - Tie each point to the reader’s real challenges.

4. **Head-to-Head Comparison Table**
   - Include a simple Markdown table comparing:
     - Platform support.
     - Management style (DIY vs fully managed).
     - Push notifications (limits and fees).
     - Revenue share.
     - Customization and flexibility.
     - Tech debt and ongoing maintenance.
   - Keep language neutral and factual.

5. **Final Thoughts**
   - Summarize the main trade-offs.
   - Reiterate why MobiLoud is the best alternative for established brands.

## Comparison Article Checklist

Before publishing a comparison article, confirm:

- Tone is conversational, confident, and direct.
- Paragraphs are short and easy to scan.
- Competitor descriptions:
  - Are fair and factual.
  - Match what their websites say.
  - Do not attack support or contracts without proof.
- For Shopify-focused builders:
  - Revenue share and performance fees are mentioned accurately where relevant.
- All nine selling points for MobiLoud appear in a meaningful way.
- Messaging is tailored to Shopify or multi-platform contexts as appropriate.
- “Shopify apps” terminology is used correctly.
- A head-to-head comparison table is included.
- The dual call to action is present.
- Required metadata fields are provided.
